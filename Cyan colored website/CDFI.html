CDFI

Center for Digitial Financial Inclusion (CDFI) was established in September 2014 with funding support from Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, Michael and Susan Dell Foundation, Omidyar Network, Tata Trusts, and institutional support from Institute for Financial Management and Research (IFMR). Since then, CDFI has grown into an innovative and vibrant organization with offices in Delhi and Bangalore. The primary objective of the Centre is to reduce inequities in financial access and services using technology. The Centre, therefore, has been developing, testing, and scaling innovative digital solutions in collaboration with its partners. CDFI's tech partners for innovations are typically small agile firms and start-ups and it works with the larger public and private partners for testing and scaling. Some solutions are being implemented as part of large scale and impactful government programs.
