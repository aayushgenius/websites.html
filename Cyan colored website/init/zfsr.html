Addressing Malnutrition in UP

Intervention in association with HarvestPlus à seed and grain production of Zn fortified wheat and Iron fortified Pearl Millet


Uttar Pradesh (UP) is the most populous state in India and has one of the highest rates of malnutrition in the world – half the children under age of 5 years have stunted linear growth. In addition to its high poverty rates, UP’s undernutrition rates are among the highest in the world.

To address malnutrition and undernutrition in Uttar Pradesh, UPPRO, in association with Harvest Plus, has initiated work on production of Zn fortified wheat seeds and grains. Till now total production of 400 quintals of Zn fortified wheat seeds and grains production has been completed.

Harvest Plus leads a global effort to improve nutrition and public health by developing and disseminating staple food crops that are rich in vitamins and minerals. Harvest Plus is a part of the CGIAR research programme on Agriculture for Nutrition and Health. Harvest Plus improves nutrition and public health in India by promoting pearl millet that provides more iron and wheat that provides more zinc in the diet. Harvest Plus and its partners work in five states of India to promote the availability, adoption, and consumption of biofortified pearl millet and wheat.
